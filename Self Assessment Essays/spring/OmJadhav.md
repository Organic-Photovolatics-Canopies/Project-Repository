# Senior Design Project Self-Assessment

**Om Jadhav**

The first semester was a period of intense learning and team-building. With no prior background in chemistry or advanced machine learning, I had to quickly familiarize myself with the fundamentals of OPV materials, power conversion efficiency (PCE), and related technologies. Our team struggled initially with data sourcing; we spent considerable time researching and identifying potential datasets, only to find many were incomplete or inaccessible. This led to iterative planning sessions where we redefined our objectives to align with available resources.


In the second semester, the project gained more structure, and I took on a more proactive role in providing technical direction. Recognizing the need for leadership, I stepped up to develop the project's architecture, delegate tasks, create data pipelines, write skeleton code, and prepare reports for team review. My responsibilities included designing the overall workflow for our machine learning models, particularly focusing on predictive modeling for PCE and later shifting to a Variational Graph Autoencoder (VGAE) for material generation. I was instrumental in setting up the technical infrastructure, including data preprocessing pipelines, model training scripts, and evaluation frameworks. This involved learning advanced concepts in graph neural networks, deep learning, and data scraping—skills I developed through self-study and collaboration with teammates. For instance, I worked on scraping patent data from sources like Lens.org to enrich our dataset and provide insights into current OPV research trends.

This project significantly enhanced my technical and soft skills. Technically, I gained proficiency in Python for data science, machine learning libraries like PyTorch and scikit-learn, and tools for data processing and visualization. I also improved my ability to design scalable architectures and manage complex workflows. On the interpersonal side, I developed stronger leadership and communication skills. Organizing the team and delegating tasks taught me how to motivate others and resolve conflicts. I learned the importance of documentation and regular check-ins to maintain project momentum.
