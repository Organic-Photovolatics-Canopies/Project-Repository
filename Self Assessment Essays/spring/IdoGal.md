Senior Design Project Self-Assessment

Ido Gal

I entered this project with limited background knowledge in both chemistry and machine learning, which made the initial stages particularly challenging. During the first semester, my primary focus was on understanding the scope of the project, conducting background research, identifying relevant data sources, and helping define a realistic plan. I made a strong effort to bring the team together around a clear and achievable direction, ensuring that our goals aligned with the time and resources available. This phase required me to develop skills in research, problem framing, and project planning, as well as communication and coordination within a diverse team.

In the second semester, our work became more structured, and I took on more technical responsibilities. I was responsible for developing a predictive model for power conversion efficiency (PCE) using the available dataset. As the project evolved, we shifted direction toward building a Variational Graph Autoencoder (VGAE) model to generate new materials, which required me to quickly learn and apply concepts in deep learning and graph-based modeling. Additionally, I worked on scraping patent data to gain a deeper understanding of organic photovoltaic (OPV) technologies and incorporate that information into our analysis. My biggest obstacle was the steep learning curve due to my limited prior experience, but I addressed this by collaborating closely with a more experienced teammate and independently studying relevant concepts. Through this process, I significantly improved my technical skills in machine learning, data processing, and model development, as well as my ability to adapt to changing project directions.
